Are AI Models Making Us Earn Money We Don't Deserve?

  • Baha Erdoğan
  • Artificial intelligence
  • 0

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is reshaping the business landscape, sparking concerns among those who have spent years equating hard work with deserved compensation. Many professionals worry that AI tools are diminishing the effort required in their jobs, leading to questions about the value of their labor and whether they are earning the money they truly deserve.

The AI Revolution: A Double-Edged Sword
As a computer engineer, I frequently delve into AI-related content. Most discussions emphasize AI's capabilities, limitations, integration processes, training data, and technological advancements. However, a significant portion of the conversation is dominated by the fear that AI might replace human jobs. But are we overlooking the human ability to adapt and shape the world?

The Human Experience: Beyond Automation
What we should focus on is not just the capabilities of AI but the human experiences that define our work. Reflecting on my own career, I've observed that many people grapple with finding their place in the early stages of their professional journeys. Some envision a brief tenure in their current roles, while others aspire to climb the corporate ladder. Yet, the reality often diverges from these expectations, leading to a mix of acceptance and silent mourning of unfulfilled dreams. Over time, individuals who have invested years in their careers develop a deep attachment to their work. Despite any inner turmoil, they continue to show up and perform their duties. This connection to their jobs grows stronger with each experience.

AI and the Perceived Devaluation of Work
The introduction of AI tools into business processes can be unsettling for those who have dedicated themselves to their work. Watching tasks that once required significant effort being completed swiftly by AI can evoke feelings of inadequacy. There's a growing concern that the money once considered well-earned may no longer be justified. This sentiment might explain the resistance to AI adoption among certain professionals.

Do We Deserve Less in the Age of AI?
The short answer is no. We are currently in a transitional phase, and the full impact of AI on our lives is yet to be determined. However, I believe that once AI tools are fully integrated, our workload will decrease further, but these tools will remain just that—tools. Consider the example of power drills, which made plumbers' work easier without reducing their earnings. Similarly, the advent of computers has lightened workloads across various industries without diminishing income. In fact, as productivity has increased, so have earnings, and we feel justified in this increase.

Embracing Change While Maintaining Quality
As with any technological advancement, the key lies in maintaining a results-oriented mindset. While it's important to enjoy the process, we should avoid becoming overly dependent on it. The quality of work remains paramount, and adapting to change is crucial for long-term success.
